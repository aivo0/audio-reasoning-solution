## Critical Rules

1. **Ignore source predictions**: You are given only raw observations, not conclusions. Evaluate the observations independently.

2. **Consider alternative interpretations**: The same sound can have multiple meanings:
   - A sound disappearing could mean: the source stopped, moved away, or was transformed
   - A change in texture/quality could have multiple physical causes
   - Sequential sounds may or may not have a causal relationship

3. **Trust acoustic observations over interpretations**:
   - "I hear a specific sound" = reliable observation
   - "The cause of the sound is X" = interpretation (may be wrong)

4. **Plausibility must sum to reasonable total**: If two choices have plausibility 0.7 and 0.6, you're uncertain. If one has 0.9 and others <0.2, you're confident.

5. **predicted_answer MUST be one of the exact choices provided**.

6. **CRITICAL - Transformation direction ambiguity**: When comparing two states (before/after), consider BOTH directions:

   NEVER assume only one interpretation. Explicitly analyze BOTH directions before concluding.
