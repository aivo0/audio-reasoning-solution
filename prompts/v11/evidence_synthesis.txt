## Your Synthesis Tasks

### 1. Timing Correction
Convert relative timing references to ABSOLUTE times based on each listener's time window:
- "at the start" from a listener covering 0.0s-X.0s -> specific time near 0.0s
- "in the middle" from a listener covering X.0s-Y.0s -> specific time around (X+Y)/2
- "at the end" from a listener covering Y.0s-Z.0s -> specific time near Z.0s
- Keep full audio timing references as-is (they're already absolute)

### 2. Cross-Listener Corroboration & Conflict Resolution
For each distinct claim, check whether it appears in the full audio analysis, focused listener analyses, or both.

- **Corroborated (focused listener + full audio agree):** Emit ONE merged observation. Add ~0.10 relevance_score bonus. In relevance_reasoning, note "Corroborated by multiple listeners". The full-audio listener independently corroborates what the focused listener detected.

- **Focused-listener-only (focused listener detects something full audio missed):** Keep the observation at baseline confidence. In relevance_reasoning, note "Detected by focused listener only". The focused listener concentrated on a narrow time window; the full-audio listener may have missed it while processing the whole recording.

- **Full-audio-only (full audio detects something no focused listener mentions):** Keep at baseline confidence. In relevance_reasoning, note "Detected in full audio only".

- **Timing conflicts (full audio and focused listener disagree on timing):** Use the focused listener's timing. The focused listener was concentrated on a narrow window and provides more precise temporal information. In relevance_reasoning, note "Timing from focused listener (narrow window)".

- **Conflicting descriptions:** When full audio and a focused listener genuinely disagree on what is heard (not just timing), note both perspectives in relevance_reasoning and keep the observation with slightly lower confidence. Different listeners hear different non-overlapping portions, so disagreement may reflect genuine variation across the audio.

- **Multiple focused-listener confirmations:** If 2+ focused listeners report the same event (rare — only if an event spans a listener boundary), add ~0.05 bonus and note "Confirmed by multiple focused listeners".

- DO NOT preserve both sides of a resolved conflict as separate observations.

### 3. Relevance Scoring
Score each observation's relevance to the SPECIFIC QUESTION (not general importance):

| 0.3-0.49 | Marginally relevant background | Q: "What emotion?" -> "Recording quality is clear" |
| 0.0-0.29 | Not relevant to the question | Q: "What emotion?" -> "Background has traffic noise" |

Scoring adjustments for corroboration:
- Corroborated by focused listener + full audio: +0.10 bonus (cap at 1.0)
- Corroborated by 2+ focused listeners (rare, spans listener boundary): +0.05 bonus
- Single source only: no adjustment

### 4. Hedging Separation
When Qwen says "X (but could be Y)", this is TWO observations:
1. Primary: "X" - score based on acoustic evidence strength
2. Caveat: "Could alternatively be Y" - score 0.2-0.4 (low relevance caveat)

Do NOT embed caveats inside primary observations. The downstream agent needs clear signals, not hedged claims.

Example:
  Input: "Speaker 2 has lower volume, possibly indicating greater distance,
          but could stem from microphone placement"
  Output observation 1: "Speaker 2 has lower perceived volume than Speaker 1"

  Output observation 2: "Volume difference could be due to microphone placement rather than distance"
    relevance_score: 0.30, relevance_reasoning: "Alternative explanation caveat - not based on specific acoustic evidence"

1. **OUTPUT ONLY JSON** - No explanatory text before or after the JSON
2. **NO RECOMMENDED ANSWER** - You are scoring observations, not answering the question
3. **RELEVANCE TO QUESTION** - Score based on the specific question, not general importance
4. **PRESERVE DETAIL** - Don't over-summarize; specific details are valuable
5. **CONFIDENCE** reflects evidence completeness:
   - 0.8+ : Clear, consistent observations covering the question

9. **TRUST FOCUSED TIMING** - When full audio and focused listener disagree on when something occurs, use the focused listener's timing. Focused listeners have a narrow listening window.
